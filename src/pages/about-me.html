About Me

GitHub · LinkedIn

I started programming journey at 13 years old by creating foosball game using the Construct game engine, at the time I thought I was going to be a professional football (soccer) player, I don't think pre-highschool me envisioned sitting at a desk all day and having back problems as a 22 y.o, but here I am, and I don't regret a thing. I didn't know I wanted to be software engineer until about halfway through my final year of highschool, at this time I was completing an introductory programming unit at Flinders University (talk about how you were fortunate enough to study a uni unit in highschool); Java of all languages was what made me realise I wanted to spend at significant amount of my life looking at a screen. After picking a degree, I had a choose a University, I still clearly remember some Monash affilates coming to my school and describing how good Monash is, of course when talking about accomodation they showed the expensive apartments, so I was somewhat suprised when I rocked up to a... charming 1960's building; however, I ended up forging many of my closest relationships in this place, so I look back upon it fondly. I enjoyed each year of university more than the last, I felt like I was learning a lot and my programming was improving rapidly, of course when I started my first proper project I realised how little I actually knew; however, this only encouraged me, I could only imagine how high the skill cieling is if it took me 3 years of full time study to not suck.

I have been working in the industry for about a year, and I already feel like I have learned so much, and I can't wait to learn and build more.
